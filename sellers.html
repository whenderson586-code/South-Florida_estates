Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding your options.

Do I have to offer compensation to a buyer's agent? +

Compensation is negotiable and is not set by law. Any request for buyer-broker compensation may be evaluated with the other financial and contractual terms of an offer, subject to applicable agreements, laws and MLS requirements.

Will my property appear on major real estate websites? +

Eligible MLS listings are distributed through the participating MLS syndication network. Publication, timing and display are controlled by each participating website and platform.

Can I make changes after the property goes live? +

Yes. Price changes, property updates and open-house information may be submitted for review and added to the MLS as appropriate.

What happens when I receive an offer? +

You can evaluate the purchase price, financing, inspection terms, requested concessions, closing date and other conditions. Broker guidance is available according to the services included in your listing agreement.

Are the upfront investments refundable? +

Listing setup and media investments cover services performed before the property is marketed and are generally non-refundable, as specified in the signed listing agreement.
